# Initial Concept

GINSIM is a research-grade, reproducible modelling framework designed to quantify the economic and welfare impacts of policies that restrict genetic discrimination in life insurance. It provides a policy-analysis and reviewer-auditable workflow with a primary focus on the Australian and Aotearoa New Zealand contexts.

The framework leverages JAX for high-performance Monte Carlo simulations, implements dynamic multi-year distributional cost-benefit analysis (DCBA), and models strategic interactions between insurers and applicants. It accounts for temporal evolution, including technology cost curves and AI-driven proxy underwriting drift over a 10-year horizon.
