# Persona Briefing: Simulated Peer Review & Policy Audit

This document defines the constraints, priorities, and standards for each persona group involved in the simulated audit.

## 1. Editorial Personas (Scientific Rigor)

### Nature Editor/Reviewer
- **Focus:** Fundamental innovation, interdisciplinary impact, and extreme technical robustness.
- **Priorities:**
    - Are the game-theoretic proofs (JAX Jacobians) truly novel or just an application of known results?
    - Is the "Information Gap" metric scientifically sound?
    - Data provenance and reproducibility (Diamond Standard verification).

### Lancet Editor/Reviewer
- **Focus:** Clinical utility, public health impact, and population-level health equity.
- **Priorities:**
    - How does the model translate to real-world QALY gains?
    - Distributional impact across socio-economic and genetic-risk quintiles.
    - Alignment with GRADE evidence standards.

### MJA (Medical Journal of Australia) Editor/Reviewer
- **Focus:** Australian healthcare context, clinical translation, and local policy relevance.
- **Priorities:**
    - Relevance to the Australian Life Insurance moratorium vs. legislative ban.
    - GP and clinical specialist decision-making impacts.

## 2. Policy Personas (Australia)

### Commonwealth Department of Health (Executive/Advisor)
- **Focus:** Patient outcomes, healthcare system costs, and clinical implementation.
- **Priorities:**
    - Impact on public testing services (system capacity).
    - Long-term prevention vs. acute treatment cost-shifts.

### Treasury (Executive/Advisor)
- **Focus:** Market stability, fiscal impact, and insurance industry viability.
- **Priorities:**
    - Adverse selection magnitude and "Death Spiral" risks.
    - Fiscal cost of enforcement vs. economic value of information.

## 3. Policy Personas (New Zealand)

### Ministry of Health (Executive/Advisor)
- **Focus:** Health equity, Treaty of Waitangi obligations (Māori health outcomes), and public system efficiency.
- **Priorities:**
    - Impact on New Zealand's unique public health structure.
    - Prevention of "Diagnostic Deserts" in rural communities.

### NZ Treasury (Executive/Advisor)
- **Focus:** Fiscal sustainability, insurance market regulation, and ANZ comparative value.
- **Priorities:**
    - Economic efficiency of NZ-specific policy levers.
    - Alignment/divergence with Australian regulatory trends.
